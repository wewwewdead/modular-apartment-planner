The reason your current movement feels restricted to one direction is likely because the math is currently set up as a simple "z-subtraction" loop (moving forward) rather than a true 3D Vector Space.To feel like you are truly "in space," we need to move the camera across all three axes ($x, y, z$) and rotate the viewport based on your input.Here is the architectural fix to give you 360-degree freedom and visible labels.1. Omni-Directional "Rocket" MathInstead of just moving the stars toward you, we need to track the Rocket's Heading.The Rotation: We track pitch (up/down) and yaw (left/right).The Velocity: We calculate a 3D vector. If you press "Forward," you move toward your current yaw/pitch.The Projection: We subtract the Rocket's Position from the Star's Position before applying the depth math.$$x_{relative} = x_{star} - x_{rocket}$$$$y_{relative} = y_{star} - y_{rocket}$$$$z_{relative} = z_{star} - z_{rocket}$$2. The "Labels in Space" ProblemStar names are currently invisible probably because they are either too small or "culled" for performance.The Fix: Labels should only appear when a star is within a "Readable Range" (e.g., $z < 5$).Billboarding: In 3D games, this is called "Billboarding." The text must always face the camera and its fontSize must scale with the star's scale.
