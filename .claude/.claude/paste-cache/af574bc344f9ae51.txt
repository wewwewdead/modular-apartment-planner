Implement Scalable Embedding-Based Galaxy Gravity

Goal: Use embeddings (vectors) to position stars and galaxies. Implement a "Centroid" strategy to ensure performance at scale.

1. The "Galaxy Centroid" Logic:

For each Galaxy (User), calculate a Mean Embedding (the average of all their posts).

Gravity Step A (Galaxy-to-Galaxy): Use Cosine Similarity between Galaxy Mean Embeddings to position the "Galaxy Suns" on the map. If two users write about similar topics, their entire galaxies should be pulled closer together.

Gravity Step B (Star-to-Galaxy): Individual stars within a galaxy should be pulled toward the Centroids of other galaxies they are similar to, causing the galaxy shape to "stretch" semantically.

2. Scaling Strategy (Spatial Partitioning):

Sectoring: Divide the infinite map into "Sectors" (e.g., 2000px blocks).

Pruning: Only calculate gravity for stars within the user's current Sector and the 8 surrounding Sectors.

Worker-Ready: Write the similarity math as a pure function so it can easily be moved to a Web Worker later if the UI thread lags.

3. Technical Implementation:

Cosine Similarity: Provide a robust JS function to calculate similarity between two float arrays.

Force-Directed Settle: Use a "Cooling" effect. When the universe loads, stars move quickly toward their semantic targets, then slow down and "lock" into place to save CPU.

Supabase Integration: Assume the embeddings are already fetched. Provide a placeholder to save the "Settled" x, y coordinates back to Supabase using a debounced RPC call.

4. Visuals:

Maintain the Andromeda/Milky Way glow.

Add "Semantic Bridges": Draw a very faint, glowing line (opacity 0.1) between stars that have a similarity score > 0.9.

Why this is the "Co-Founder" Winning Move:
Efficiency: By calculating the "Average" of a galaxy, we reduce the math from millions of calculations to just a few dozen.

Organization: This naturally creates Neighborhoods. You’ll have a "Tech Sector," a "Poetry Nebula," and an "Academic Cluster" without ever having to manually categorize them.

The "Vibe" Shift: When User #4 joins and writes their first post, the "Centroid" of their new galaxy will immediately feel the "Tug" of your 3 existing galaxies, and the new user will see their home "flying" toward the people they relate to most.
